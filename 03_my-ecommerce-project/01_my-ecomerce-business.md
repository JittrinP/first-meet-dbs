## 1. Describe the human problem
-1.Many customer has problem about they don't know current vehicel has problem or not, 
-2.They cannot estimate the price that they need to prepare. so, i need to know what part they need to change and how much they need to prepare. currently , customer need to compare with other shop.
-3.Existing tools only cover scheduled maintenance, not real-time problems
-4.You need to already know the part name/code to search for it: Existing parts catalogs
-5.No guidance on urgency or consequences: There's no information to help riders decide whether an issue needs immediate attention or can wait

## 2. Describe how your solution solves these problems
-1.Symptom-based diagnosis
-2.Symptom → part, directly: Users don't need to know any part names or codes upfront. 
-3.Severity level + consequences if ignored: Each recommendation comes with an urgency level (e.g., "replace immediately" vs. "can wait a bit") and an explanation of what could happen if the issue isn't addressed, so users can make an informed decision themselves.
-4.One continuous flow: From symptom input all the way to seeing the part's price, adding it to cart, and checking out — all in a single flow, with no need to visit a service center just to ask or buy.
-5.Data that compounds over time: Every symptom problem is record (vehicle model, reported symptom, recommended part), building a proprietary dataset that can later be analyzed — for example, spotting trends in common issues by model or region.

## 3. Describe your ecommerce business idea including core features
-1.Core concept: A single-vendor online store (not a marketplace) selling Honda motorcycle parts, differentiated by a built-in symptom-diagnosis engine that guides customers to the right part before they buy.
-2.Scope: Honda motorcycles only
-3.Feature :
    1.Browse products
    2.Symptom Diagnosis (key differentiator)
    3.Add to cart
    4.Place order
    5.Pay
    6.Admin dashboard : Manage products, manage orders, and maintain the symptom-cause-part knowledge base
-4.Target customers: Honda motorcycle owners with little to no mechanical knowledge, and DIY riders who want to save on labor costs by fixing their own bikes.
